Hello : )
Hi there, welcome to my page. My name is Kiran Delneuville, and I study PACS and computer things at UC Berkeley. I wanted to create a page that would not only serve as an online presence, but to help explain my background and the places that I have been fortunate enough to call home.
Each orange dot on the map below represents places that I either still, or once called home. I attended high school in Chiang Mai, Thailand; which is what the left-most dot in Southeast Asia represents. Following that, I worked as a consultant for various NGO's in Vientiane, the capital of neighbouring Laos. I then attended the University of New South Wales in Sydney, Australia, before finally moving to the Bay Area, a place I am happy to call home.
Below is a sample of some of the work that I have completed for several Non-Governmental Organizations during my time working in Laos. These organizations include Handicap International, COPE, the National Rehabilitation Center and the Conference on Cluster Munitions.
'A Different Outlook' - Art Book
In late 2010, a very significant conference on the topic of cluster munitions was set to take place in the Lao capital of Vientiane. This milestone was to attract heads of state from various nations and dozens of high ranking officials along with United Nations leaders. In commemoration of this event, we gathered the best artists nationwide, took them along to see certain UXO (un-exploded ordnants) affected areas and then asked them to depict what they saw through their art. The result was more than we ever imagined, with over hundred unique art pieces produced, which were subsequentially displayed during the opening ceremony of the conference. The following are pages taken from an art catalogue I was in charge of designing which was then sold in order to raise awareness and funds for victims of UXO.
Handicap International Mission Statement Publication
